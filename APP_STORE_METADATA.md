## Description

You have roughly 4,160 weeks on this planet. How many have you already spent?

Your Countdown turns an ancient philosophical practice into a daily companion. Memento mori — "remember that you will die" — is not about fear. It is about clarity. When you see your remaining time laid bare in seconds, sunrises, and summers, something shifts. The trivial falls away. What matters comes into focus.

Open the app and watch your life tick down in real time. Years, months, weeks, days, hours, minutes, seconds — all flowing steadily forward. It is not meant to alarm you. It is meant to wake you up.

VISUALIZE YOUR ENTIRE LIFE
See your weeks as a grid of 4,160 dots — filled and unfilled. View your time as a spiral unwinding, a calendar filling, or decade blocks stacking. Each visualization tells the same truth from a different angle: your time is limited, and that is what makes it valuable.

LIFE IN NUMBERS
Discover what remains in terms you actually feel. How many more sunrises will you witness? How many summers? How many meals shared with the people you love? How many full moons, heartbeats, breaths? These are not abstract statistics. They are your life, counted.

STAND AMONG GIANTS
Mozart died at 35. Bruce Lee at 32. Steve Jobs built Apple, lost it, and rebuilt it before his time ran out. See where you stand compared to those who made their finite years count. The question is not how long you have — it is what you do with what remains.

DAILY REMINDERS
A single push notification each day. Not nagging. Not anxious. Just a quiet reminder that today is one you will not get back.

CUSTOM MILESTONES
Track countdowns to the moments that define your year — a wedding, a move, a goal, a deadline. See them alongside your life countdown for perspective on what truly matters.

SHARE YOUR PERSPECTIVE
Send your life stats to a friend. Start a conversation most people avoid but everyone needs.

Built with a dark, minimalist design because the numbers speak for themselves. No clutter. No gamification. No ads. No tracking.

The Stoics practiced memento mori to live better, not to despair. This app exists in that tradition. Your life is not infinite. That is not sad. That is the entire point.

## App Review Notes

Your Countdown is based on "memento mori," a philosophical practice originating with the ancient Stoics and adopted across many wisdom traditions. The Latin phrase means "remember that you will die" and is used as a tool for motivation, gratitude, and intentional living. It is not morbid, nihilistic, or related to self-harm in any way.

The app is comparable to journaling and mindfulness apps in its intent. It encourages users to reflect on the finite nature of time in order to prioritize what matters to them. The concept is widely discussed in mainstream books (Ryan Holiday's "The Daily Stoic"), TED talks, and wellness publications. Existing apps in this space (WeCroak, Time Left) have been approved and featured on the App Store.

No graphic or disturbing content is presented. The tone throughout is calm, reflective, and motivational.
